                         7. EXCEPTION ANALYSIS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Schedule B-II exceptions are analyzed below:

EXCEPTION 1: TAXES
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"Standby fees, taxes and assessments by any taxing authority for the year
2025, and subsequent years."

CLASSIFICATION: Standard Pre-Printed Exception
IMPACT: Low - Standard exception in all policies
RECOMMENDATION: No action required. Taxes prorated at closing.

EXCEPTION 2: RESTRICTIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"Restrictive Covenants recorded in Volume 1234, Page 567, Deed Records,
Travis County, Texas, but omitting any covenant or restriction based on race,
color, religion, sex, handicap, familial status, or national origin."

CLASSIFICATION: Deed Restrictions (CCRs)
ANALYSIS: These are the Oak Hills Subdivision restrictions. Per the HOA
          resale certificate, the restrictions include:
          - Single-family residential use only
          - Minimum 2,000 sq ft living area
          - 25-foot front setback, 10-foot side setbacks
          - Architectural review for exterior modifications
          - No commercial vehicles visible from street

IMPACT: Medium - Restrictions affect property use. Current improvements
        appear to comply based on survey showing setbacks.
RECOMMENDATION: Remains as exception. Buyer should review restrictions
               for compatibility with intended use.

EXCEPTION 3: UTILITY EASEMENT
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"Ten (10) foot utility easement along the rear property line as shown on the
recorded plat."

CLASSIFICATION: Utility Easement (Platted)
ANALYSIS: Survey shows 10-foot easement along rear (north) property line.
          No structures encroach into easement. Underground utilities present.
IMPACT: Low - Easement in expected location; no improvements affected.
RECOMMENDATION: Remains as exception. Standard utility easement.

EXCEPTION 4: MINERAL RESERVATION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
"Reservation of oil, gas and other minerals by Oak Hills Development Co.
in deed recorded Volume 987, Page 654, Deed Records."

CLASSIFICATION: Mineral Reservation
ANALYSIS: Original subdivision developer reserved all minerals. Reservation
          includes standard ingress/egress rights for mineral exploration.
          No active mineral leases found of record.
IMPACT: Medium - Minerals severed from surface. Surface use rights reserved
        to mineral owner per dominant mineral estate doctrine.
RECOMMENDATION: Remains as exception. Standard for this subdivision.
               Buyer should understand mineral estate is separate.

                      8. TEXAS-SPECIFIC MATTERS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

HOMESTEAD STATUS:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
The property is owned by an LLC and is not eligible for homestead protection
under Texas law. No homestead analysis required.

COMMUNITY PROPERTY:
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Not applicable - property owned by entity, not individuals.

MECHANIC'S LIEN EXPOSURE (Per Standards 15.70-15.80):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Texas Constitution Art. XVI, § 37 creates a constitutional mechanic's lien
that attaches regardless of recording. Per the survey dated [Date], no
evidence of recent construction. Recommend T-47 affidavit to confirm no
improvements within 90 days.

RECORDING STATUTE ANALYSIS (Standard 15.90):
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Texas has a NOTICE recording system (Tex. Prop. Code § 13.001). A subsequent
good-faith purchaser for value prevails over prior unrecorded interests if
the purchaser had no actual, constructive, or inquiry notice. No unrecorded
interests were discovered in this examination, though this draft does not
include an independent search of possession or physical inspection.

## Usage Notes

1. **Adapt to Facts**: Every property is different. Use this as a structure, not a template to copy.

2. **Expand Sections as Needed**: Complex issues need more discussion.

3. **Cite Standards**: Reference specific Texas Title Examination Standards for key conclusions.

4. **Flag Uncertainties**: If something is unclear, say so explicitly.

5. **Recommend Follow-Up**: Identify what additional investigation may be needed.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EXCEPTION 1: TAXES AND ASSESSMENTS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

COMMITMENT LANGUAGE:
"Standby fees, taxes, assessments, and charges by any taxing authority for
the year 2025 and subsequent years, and subsequent taxes and assessments by
any taxing authority for prior years due to change in land usage or ownership."

CLASSIFICATION: Standard Pre-Printed Exception

ANALYSIS:
This is a standard exception appearing in all Texas title commitments. It
covers:
- Current year taxes not yet assessed
- Future years' taxes
- Rollback taxes if land use changes

Per Standard 15.10, ad valorem property taxes have first-priority lien status
under TEX. TAX CODE § 32.01.

DOCUMENTS REVIEWED:
- Tax Certificate dated [Date] showing 2024 taxes PAID
- 2025 taxes not yet due

IMPACT ASSESSMENT: LOW
- Standard exception; cannot be deleted
- 2025 taxes estimated at $8,500 (prorate at closing)
- No delinquent taxes
- No rollback tax exposure (no ag exemption history)

RECOMMENDATION: No action required
- Exception remains as standard
- Prorate current year taxes at closing
- Verify no special assessments pending

CURE: N/A (standard exception)


──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EXCEPTION 2: RIGHTS OF PARTIES IN POSSESSION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

COMMITMENT LANGUAGE:
"Rights of parties in possession not shown by the public records."

CLASSIFICATION: Standard Pre-Printed Exception (Deletable)

ANALYSIS:
This exception protects against unrecorded possessory rights including:
- Tenants under unrecorded leases
- Adverse possessors
- Licensees

Per Standard 3.40, an examiner should consider evidence of possession that
might create inquiry notice.

DOCUMENTS REVIEWED:
- Survey shows no evidence of third-party occupation
- Seller affidavit confirms owner-occupied, no tenants

IMPACT ASSESSMENT: LOW
- Property is owner-occupied (by seller's principals)
- No evidence of adverse possession
- No tenants or licensees

RECOMMENDATION: Request deletion with documentation

CURE STEPS:
1. [ ] Obtain seller's affidavit stating:
       - No parties in possession other than seller
       - No unrecorded leases or tenancies
       - No boundary disputes or adverse claims
2. [ ] Survey shows no evidence of third-party possession
3. [ ] Title company approves deletion

STATUS: [ ] Affidavit obtained  [ ] Deletion approved

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
EXCEPTION 4: MECHANIC'S AND MATERIALMAN'S LIENS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

COMMITMENT LANGUAGE:
"Any lien, or right to a lien, for services, labor, or material heretofore
or hereafter furnished, imposed by law and not shown by the public records."

CLASSIFICATION: Standard Pre-Printed Exception (Deletable)

ANALYSIS:
Texas Constitution Art. XVI, § 37 creates a self-executing constitutional
lien for labor and materials. Per Standards 15.70-15.80:
- Lien attaches regardless of recording
- Inception date: when work commenced or materials delivered
- Perfection: affidavit filed within statutory period
- May prime recorded deeds of trust

DOCUMENTS REVIEWED:
- Survey dated [Date] shows no evidence of recent construction
- T-47 affidavit to confirm no improvements within 90 days

IMPACT ASSESSMENT: LOW (assuming no recent work)
- No visible construction activity per survey
- Property appears stable (no additions/modifications)

RECOMMENDATION: Delete with T-47 affidavit

CURE STEPS:
1. [ ] Seller executes T-47 affidavit certifying:
       - No improvements, alterations, or repairs within 90 days
       - No work contracted for but not yet performed
       - No unpaid bills for prior work
2. [ ] If recent work: Obtain lien waivers from all contractors
3. [ ] Title company approves deletion

IF RECENT CONSTRUCTION:
- Obtain all contracts and payment records
- Obtain final lien waivers from:
  [ ] General contractor
  [ ] All subcontractors
  [ ] All material suppliers
- Consider lien escrow for protection period

STATUS: [ ] T-47 obtained  [ ] Deletion approved

EXCEPTION 8: MINERAL RESERVATION
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

COMMITMENT LANGUAGE:
"All oil, gas, and other minerals in, on, and under the Property together

DOCUMENT REVIEWED: Deed containing mineral reservation, Vol. 987, Pg. 654

- Reserving Party: Oak Hills Development Co. (developer)
- Surface Rights Reserved: Yes - ingress/egress for mineral operations
- Depth Limitation: None stated (all depths)
- Pooling Rights: Reserved
- Executive Rights: Reserved (right to lease)

CURRENT STATUS:
- Railroad Commission records: No active wells on property
- No current mineral lease of record for this tract
- Adjacent tracts: [Check for nearby production]

DOMINANT MINERAL ESTATE:
Per Standard 20.10, the mineral owner has implied right to use surface
as reasonably necessary for mineral extraction. This may include:
- Access roads
- Well sites
- Pipeline routes
- Storage facilities

IMPACT ASSESSMENT: MEDIUM
- Surface owner does not own minerals
- Mineral owner has surface access rights
- No current production or leasing activity
- Standard for this area/subdivision

RECOMMENDATION: Remains as exception

§ 209.0091, up to six months of assessments have "super-priority"
status over most other liens (except property taxes and prior recorded
liens for work performed).

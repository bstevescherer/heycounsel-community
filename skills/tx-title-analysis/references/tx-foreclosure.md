## Foreclosure Overview

### Texas Non-Judicial Foreclosure (Tex. Prop. Code § 51.002)

**Key requirements**:
- Deed of trust must contain power of sale
- Notice of default and acceleration
- 21-day notice before posting (certified mail + posting on property + filing with county clerk)
- 21 days between posting and sale
- Sale on first Tuesday of month, 10 AM - 4 PM
- At courthouse door or designated area

**Title implications**:
- Foreclosure extinguishes junior liens
- Senior liens survive and must be addressed
- Defective notice = voidable sale
- Trustee's deed conveys only grantor's interest at time of deed of trust

### Judicial Foreclosure

**When used**:
- Home equity loans (TEX. CONST. art. XVI, § 50)
- When deed of trust lacks power of sale
- Tax lien foreclosures
- HOA assessment liens (in some cases)

**Title considerations**:
- Court judgment required
- Longer timeline (90+ days)
- Right of redemption periods may apply
- Lis pendens should be filed (Tex. Prop. Code § 12.007)

## Deed in Lieu of Foreclosure

### Title Analysis Requirements

**CRITICAL**: Deed in lieu does NOT extinguish junior liens

**Pre-closing verification**:
1. Full title search for all encumbrances
2. Junior lienholder releases or subordination agreements
3. Judgment and tax lien searches
4. HOA assessment status
5. Mechanic's lien potential (recent improvements?)

**Documentation required**:
- Special warranty deed (or quitclaim) from borrower
- Estoppel affidavit re: no other claims
- T-47 affidavit
- Release of any personal guarantees
- 1099-C tax implications disclosure

### Junior Lien Treatment

**Options for junior liens**:
1. **Pay off**: Full satisfaction and release
2. **Negotiate release**: Discount for voluntary release
3. **Short payoff**: Partial payment accepted as full satisfaction
4. **Subordination**: Junior agrees to remain subordinate (rare)
5. **Foreclosure anyway**: If juniors won't cooperate

**Risk assessment**:
- Junior lienholder may foreclose their interest
- Title insurance may except outstanding liens
- Buyer takes subject to unreleased liens

## Short Sales

### Title Considerations

**Pre-approval requirements**:
- All lienholders must approve sale price
- Short sale approval letters from each lienholder
- Confirmation no deficiency will be pursued (or proper disclosure)

**Lien release timing**:
- Releases must be recorded at or immediately after closing
- Escrow release documents with title company
- Wire payoffs to lienholders per approval letters

### Common Short Sale Issues

**Multiple lienholders**:
- First and second mortgages may have different servicers
- Each requires separate approval
- Junior may demand minimum payment to release

**Judgment liens**:
- May not be included in short sale approval
- Require separate negotiation
- Full payoff or court order to clear

**Tax liens**:
- Federal tax liens: IRS 120-day redemption right survives
- State tax liens: Must be addressed in approval
- Property tax liens: Always senior, must be current

## Lien Priority and Marshaling

### Texas Recording Statute (Tex. Prop. Code § 13.001)

Texas has a **NOTICE** recording system (Standard 15.90):
- A subsequent good-faith purchaser for value prevails over a prior unrecorded instrument
- The subsequent purchaser prevails even if the prior instrument records first
- Notice (actual, constructive, or inquiry) defeats the subsequent purchaser's claim

**Requirements for protection**:
1. Good faith purchaser
2. For valuable consideration
3. Without notice of the prior interest at time of purchase

### Priority Rules

**General priority** (first in time, first in right with notice exceptions):

| Lien Type | Priority Notes |
|-----------|---------------|
| Property tax liens | Always first (constitutional/statutory) |
| Federal tax liens | From date of assessment if filed |
| Mechanic's liens | Relate back to inception of work (constitutional) |
| Deed of trust | From date of recording |
| Judgment liens | From date of recording abstract |
| HOA liens | 6-month super-priority (Tex. Prop. Code § 209.0091) |

### Texas Mechanic's Lien Priority (TEX. CONST. art. XVI, § 37)

**Constitutional lien**:
- Attaches to land regardless of recording
- Inception date: When labor/materials first furnished
- May relate back before deed of trust recording
- 4-month perfection period from completion

**Original contractor vs. subcontractor**:
- Original contractor: Direct lien rights
- Subcontractor: Must comply with notice requirements (Tex. Prop. Code §§ 53.021-53.059)

### Federal Tax Lien Priority (26 U.S.C. §§ 6321-6323)

**Filing requirements**:
- Must file in county where property located
- Notice valid for 10 years + 30 days (refiling extends)
- Priority from date of assessment if properly filed

**Superpriority claims** (beat even prior recorded liens):
- Property tax liens
- Mechanic's liens (if inception before tax lien filing)
- Purchase money security interests (45-day rule)

**120-day redemption right**:
- IRS can redeem property for 120 days after foreclosure sale
- Purchaser at foreclosure takes subject to this right
- Title insurance typically excepts during redemption period

### Transferred Tax Liens (Tex. Tax Code §§ 32.06, 32.065)

**Priority**:
- Steps into shoes of taxing unit
- Superior to all other liens except:
  - Other tax liens
  - Federal tax liens (depending on timing)

**Title analysis**:
- Verify transferred tax lien status
- Obtain payoff from transferee
- Confirm release upon satisfaction

### Equitable Subrogation

**When available**:
- Lender pays off prior lien
- Lender intended to obtain same priority
- No prejudice to intervening interests
- Other equitable factors favor subrogation

**Texas recognition**: Courts apply equitable subrogation to prevent unjust enrichment

**Common scenario**:
- Refinance lender pays off first mortgage
- New lien recorded after intervening judgment
- Refinance lender may claim subrogation to first lien priority

## Bankruptcy Impact on Title

### Automatic Stay (11 U.S.C. § 362)

**Effect on title**:
- Stops foreclosure proceedings
- Invalidates post-petition foreclosure sales
- Violations may void conveyances

**Relief from stay**:
- Motion required before foreclosure can proceed
- Stay lift order should be recorded
- Title company may require order copy

### Bankruptcy Discharge vs. Lien Survival

**Key distinction**:
- Discharge eliminates personal liability
- Liens survive discharge and remain attached to property
- Creditor can foreclose post-discharge

**Title implications**:
- Discharged debt doesn't clear liens
- Must still obtain lien releases
- Borrower may execute release without payment obligation

### Lien Avoidance (11 U.S.C. § 522)

**Homestead exemption**:
- Texas unlimited homestead exemption (acreage limits only)
- May avoid judicial liens impairing homestead
- Does not affect consensual liens (deeds of trust)

**Preference actions**:
- Transfers within 90 days of filing may be avoided
- Real estate: Recording date controls
- Title insurance may except preference period

### Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13 Considerations

**Chapter 7 (Liquidation)**:
- Trustee may abandon property if no equity
- Abandonment returns title to debtor
- Foreclosure can proceed after stay lifted

**Chapter 13 (Reorganization)**:
- Debtor keeps property, pays through plan
- Lien stripping in some circumstances
- Cure of arrearages through plan

## Common Title Defects in Distressed Properties

### Legal Description Errors

**Sufficiency test** (Zobel v. Slim, 576 S.W.2d 362):
- Description must identify land with reasonable certainty
- Parol evidence admissible to apply description to ground
- "That which is certain can be made certain"

**Common errors**:
- Lot/block transposition
- Incorrect subdivision name
- Missing metes and bounds calls
- Survey reference errors

**Cure options**:
- Correction deed
- Scrivener's affidavit
- Court reformation

### Borrower Name Errors

**Issues**:
- Name on deed differs from note/deed of trust
- Corporate/entity name variations
- Married name changes
- AKA variations

**Resolution**:
- Identity affidavit
- Correction instruments
- Name change documentation

### Spousal Joinder Issues

**Texas requirements**:
- Community property: Both spouses must join in conveyance
- Homestead: Both spouses must sign (TEX. CONST. art. XVI, § 50)
- Separate property: May require joinder disclaimer

**Foreclosure context**:
- Both spouses should be named in deed of trust
- Missing spouse signature may void lien on homestead
- Home equity loans: Strict constitutional requirements

### Prior Unreleased Liens (Tex. Prop. Code § 12.017)

**4-year release provision**:
- Lien presumed released if:
  - More than 4 years since maturity date
  - No extension recorded
  - No suit pending

**10-year provision** (if no maturity date stated):
- Lien presumed released 10 years after recording
- Same conditions as above

**Title company practice**:
- May require affidavit of non-renewal
- Research for assignments/modifications
- Consider indemnity if within period

## Post-Foreclosure Title Issues

### Deficiency of Trustee's Deed

**Common defects**:
- Wrong trustee (substitute not recorded)
- Improper notice
- Sale procedures not followed
- Bid price issues (rare in Texas)

**Remedies**:
- Void vs. voidable distinction
- Curative statutes may apply
- Re-foreclosure if necessary

### Former Owner/Tenant Possession

**Eviction requirements**:
- Formal eviction required (no self-help)
- Justice court jurisdiction
- Notice to vacate timing

**Title implications**:
- Exception for rights of parties in possession
- Possession may indicate adverse claims
- Inspection recommended before policy issuance

### Deficiency Judgments

**Texas requirements**:
- Must sue within 2 years of foreclosure
- Fair market value offset applies
- Personal liability only (doesn't affect title)

**Impact**: No direct title impact, but may lead to judgment liens on other property

### REO Property Considerations

**Lender as seller**:
- Special warranty deed typical
- AS-IS condition
- Limited representations

**Title requirements**:
- Entity authority documentation
- FDIC issues if failed bank
- Servicer authority verification

## Workout Analysis Checklist

### Pre-Transaction Title Review

- [ ] Current owner search (verify vesting)
- [ ] All deeds of trust (check recording, modifications)
- [ ] All junior liens (judgments, tax liens, HOA)
- [ ] Mechanic's lien potential (recent work?)
- [ ] Bankruptcy search (all owners/entities)
- [ ] Federal tax lien search
- [ ] State tax lien/franchise tax search
- [ ] Lis pendens search
- [ ] Survey review (if available)

### Foreclosure File Requirements

- [ ] Note and deed of trust
- [ ] Payment history
- [ ] Default/acceleration notices
- [ ] Posting/filing certificates
- [ ] Trustee's deed
- [ ] Bid documentation

### Deed in Lieu File Requirements

- [ ] Title commitment showing all liens
- [ ] Junior lienholder releases/subordinations
- [ ] Borrower affidavits (T-47, estoppel)
- [ ] Deed (special warranty or quitclaim)
- [ ] 1099-C/forgiveness documentation
- [ ] Loan modification history (if any)

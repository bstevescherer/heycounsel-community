TL;DR

State and city laws are filling a federal vacuum.

No federal statute or preemption.
Congress has not passed an AI employment law. President Trump's December 11, 2025 executive order ("Ensuring a National Policy Framework for AI") directs the DOJ to establish an "AI Litigation Task Force" charged with challenging state AI laws, and specifically names the Colorado AI Act as a problematic example. The EO itself does not preempt any state law. On April 24, 2026, the DOJ filed a statement of interest joining xAI's pending challenge to the Colorado AI Act, the first publicly disclosed Task Force action. Practitioners advise: comply with state laws, don't wait.
Five enacted laws, three already live.
NYC LL 144 (2023), California ADS regs (Oct 2025), Illinois HB 3773 (Jan 2026), and Texas TRAIGA (Jan 2026) are in effect. Colorado AI Act was delayed from Feb 2026 to June 30, 2026 by SB 25B-004.
Two shared requirements across enacted laws.
Every law touches some form of (1) notice / transparency to employees and applicants, and (2) anti-discrimination obligation, but the standard varies sharply: most laws are effects-based / disparate impact; Texas is intent-based; NYC is process-based (mandatory bias audit).

Illinois HB 3773

HB 3773 (Public Act 103-0804) · Draft Illinois Department of Human Rights (IDHR) Subpart J Rules

Illinois HB 3773 bars employers from using AI in ways that discriminate, requires notice when AI is used in employment decisions, and defines AI broadly to cover most HR tools.

Effective date
January 1, 2026. Draft rules released late 2025. Final rules pending.
Covered employers
  • Any Illinois employer with 1 or more employees for 20+ calendar weeks.
  • For disability, pregnancy, and sexual-harassment claims: 1 or more employees, no week threshold.
  • Under the draft rule: employer "agents," including recruiters and third-party vendors.
Covered decisions
Recruitment, hiring, promotion, renewal, selection for training or apprenticeship, discharge, discipline, tenure, and any terms, privileges, or conditions of employment.
AI definition
Broad: covers any software that, when prompted, returns text, image, audio, or video content.

Compliance requirements

1. Notice requirements
  • Cadence: annual for existing employees; 30 days when new tools are adopted.
  • Channels: handbook or policy doc; physical premises; intranet or external website; job postings.
  • Required content (7 items): product name and vendor; covered decision; AI purpose and data categories; positions affected; HR contact; accommodation rights; the Illinois Human Rights Act (IHRA) § 2-102(L) anti-discrimination language.
  • Retention period: 4 years.
2. Audit tools
Use of AI tools must not result in disparate impact. Intent not required. Bans use of ZIP codes as a proxy for protected classes.
